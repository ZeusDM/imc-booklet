Let \(p\) be a prime number. A flea is staying at point \(0\) of the real line. At each minute, the flea has three possibilities: to stay at its position, or to move by \(1\) to the left or to the right. After \(p-1\) minutes, it wants to be at \(0\) again. Denote by \(f(p)\) the number of its strategies to do this (for example, \(f(3)=3\): it may either stay at \(0\) for the entire time, or go to the left and then to the right, or go to the right and then to the left). Find \(f(p)\pmod p\).
