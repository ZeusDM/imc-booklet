Let \(n>3\) be an integer. Let \(\Omega\) be the set of all triples of distinct elements of \(\{1,2,\ldots,n\}\). Let \(m\) denote the minimal number of colours which suffice to colour \(\Omega\) so that whenever \(1\le a<b<c<d\le n\), the triples \(\{a,b,c\}\) and \(\{b,c,d\}\) have different colours. Prove that
\[
\frac{1}{100}\log\log n \le m \le 100\log\log n.
\]
