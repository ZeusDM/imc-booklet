Let \(a_{0}=\sqrt{2}\), \(b_{0}=2\), \(a_{n+1}=\sqrt{2-\sqrt{4-a_{n}^{2}}}\), and \(b_{n+1}=\frac{2b_{n}}{2+\sqrt{4+b_{n}^{2}}}\).
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] Prove that the sequences \((a_{n})\) and \((b_{n})\) are decreasing and converge to \(0\).
\item[(b)] Prove that the sequence \((2^{n}a_{n})\) is increasing,  the sequence \((2^{n}b_{n})\) is decreasing and
both converge to the same limit.
\item[(c)] Prove that there exists a positive constant \(C\) such that for all \(n\)  the following inequality holds: \(0 <b_{n}-a_{n} <\frac{C}{8^{n}}\).
\end{itemize}
