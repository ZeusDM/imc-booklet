Let \(A=(a_{k, l})_{k, l \in \{1, \dots, n\}}\) be a complex \(n \times n\) matrix such that for each \(m \in \{1,2,\dots,n\}\) and \(1 \leq  j_{1} <\dots<j_{m}\) the determinant of the matrix \((a_{j_{k}, j_{l}})_{k, l \in \{1, \dots, n\}}\) is zero. Prove that \(A^{n}=0\) and that there exists a permutation \(\sigma \in S_{n}\) such that the matrix \((a_{\sigma(k), \sigma(l)})_{k, l \in \{1, \dots, n\}}\) has all of its nonzero elements above the diagonal.
