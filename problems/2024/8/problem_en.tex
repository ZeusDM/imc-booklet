Define the sequence \(x_1, x_2, \ldots\) by the initial terms \(x_1 = 2\), \(x_2 = 4\), and the recurrence relation
\[
x_{n+2} = 3x_{n+1} - 2x_n + \frac{2^n}{x_n} \quad \text{for } n \ge 1.
\]
Prove that \(\displaystyle\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{x_n}{2^n}\) exists and satisfies
\[
\frac{1 + \sqrt{3}}{2} \le \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{x_n}{2^n} \le \frac{3}{2}.
\]
