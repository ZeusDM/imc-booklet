Let \(P \in \mathbb{R}[x]\) be a polynomial with real coefficients, and suppose \(\deg(P) \ge 2\). For every \(x \in \mathbb{R}\), let \(\ell_x \subset \mathbb{R}^2\) denote the line tangent to the graph of \(P\) at the point \((x, P(x))\).
\begin{itemize}
    \item[(a)] Suppose that the degree of \(P\) is odd. Show that \(\displaystyle\bigcup_{x \in \mathbb{R}} \ell_x = \mathbb{R}^2\).
    \item[(b)] Does there exist a polynomial of even degree for which the above equality still holds?
\end{itemize}
