Find all the positive integers \(n\) for  which there exists a Family \(\mathcal{F}\) of three-element subsets of \(S=\{1,2,\dots,n\}\) satisfying
\begin{itemize}
    \item[(i)] for any two different  elements \(a,b \in S\) there exists exactly one \(A \in \mathcal{F}\) containing both \(a\) and \(b\);
    \item[(ii)] if \(a,b,c,x,y,z\) are elements of \(S\) such that \(\{a,b,x\},\{a,c,y\},\{b,c,z\} \in \mathcal{F}\), then \(\{x,y,z\} \in \mathcal{F} \).
\end{itemize}
