The linear operator \(A\) on a finite-dimensional vector space \(V\) is called an involution if
\(A^{2}=I\), where \(I\) is the identity operator. Let \(\dim V=n\).
\begin{itemize}
    \item[(a)] Prove that for every involution \(A\) on \(V\), there exists a basis of \(V\) consisting of eigenvectors
    of \(A\).
    \item[(b)] Find the maximal number of distinct pairwise commuting involutions on \(V\).
\end{itemize}
