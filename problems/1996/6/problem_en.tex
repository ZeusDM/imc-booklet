Upper content of a subset \(E\) of the plane \(\mathbb{R}^{2}\) is defined as
\[\mathcal{C}(E)=\inf\left\{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \operatorname{diam}(E_{i})\right\}\]where \(\inf\) is taken over all finite families of sets \(E_{1},\dots,E_{n}\), \(n\in \mathbb{N}\), in \(\mathbb{R}^{2}\)
such that \(E\subset \bigcup_{i=1}^{n}E_{i}\).
Lower content of \(E\) is defined as
\[\mathcal{K}(E)=\sup\left\{\operatorname{length}(L) : \begin{array}{l} \text{\(L\) is a closed line segment} \\ \text{onto which \(E\) can be contracted} \end{array} \right\}.\]
Prove that
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] \(\mathcal{C}(L)=\operatorname{length}(L)\) if \(L\) is a closed line segment;
\item[(b)] \(\mathcal{C}(E) \geq \mathcal{K}(E)\);
\item[(c)] the equality in (b) is not always true even if \(E\) is compact.
\end{itemize}
