Find all \(r > 0\) such that whenever \(f \colon \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R}\) is a differentiable function such that \(|\operatorname{grad}f(0,0)| = 1\) and \(|\operatorname{grad}f(u) - \operatorname{grad} f(v)| \le |u - v|\) for all \(u,v \in \mathbb{R}^2\), then the maximum of \(f\) on the disk \(\{u \in \mathbb{R}^2 : |u| \le r\}\) is attained at exactly one point.

(\(\operatorname{grad} f(u) = (\partial_1 f(u), \partial_2 f(u))\) is the gradient vector of \(f\) at the point \(u\); for a vector \(u = (a,b)\), \(|u| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\).)
