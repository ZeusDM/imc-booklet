Given a group \(G\), denote by \(G(m)\) the subgroup generated by the \(m^{\text{th}}\) powers of elements of \(G\). If \(G(m)\) and \(G(n)\) are commutative, prove that \(G(\operatorname{gcd}(m,n))\) is also commutative. (\(\operatorname{gcd}(m,n)\) denotes the greatest common divisor of \(m\) and \(n\).)
