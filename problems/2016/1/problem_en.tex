Let \(f \colon  \left[ a, b\right]\to\mathbb{R}\) be continuous on \(\left[ a, b\right]\) and differentiable on \(\left( a, b\right)\). Suppose that \(f\) has infinitely many zeros, but there is no \(x\in \left( a, b\right)\) with \(f(x)=f'(x)=0\).
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] Prove that \(f(a)f(b)=0\).
\item[(b)] Give an example of such a function on \(\left[ 0, 1\right]\).
\end{itemize}
