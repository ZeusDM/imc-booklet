Let \(n\) be a positive integer, and denote by \(\mathbb{Z}_n\) the ring of integers modulo \(n\). Suppose that there exists a function \(f\colon \mathbb{Z}_n\to\mathbb{Z}_n\) satisfying the following three properties:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] \(f(x)\neq x\),
\item[(ii)] \(f(f(x))=x\),
\item[(iii)] \(f(f(f(x+1)+1)+1)=x\) for all \(x\in\mathbb{Z}_n\).
\end{itemize}
Prove that \(n\equiv 2 \pmod4\).
