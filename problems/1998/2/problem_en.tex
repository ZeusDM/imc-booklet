Consider the following statement: for any permutation \(\pi_1\not=\mathbb{I}\) of \(\{1,2,\dots,n\}\) there is a permutation \(\pi_2\) such that any permutation on these numbers can be obtained by a finite compostion of \(\pi_1\) and \(\pi_2\).
\begin{itemize}

\item[(a)] Prove the statement for \(n=3\) and \(n=5\).
\item[(b)] Disprove the statement for \(n=4\).
\end{itemize}
