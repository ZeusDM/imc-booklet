Let \(A\) be an \(n\)x\(n\) matrix with integer entries and \(b_{1},b_{2},\dots,b_{k}\) be integers satisfying \(detA=b_{1}\cdot b_{2}\cdot \dots\cdot b_{k}\). Prove that there exist \(n\)x\(n\)-matrices \(B_{1},B_{2},\dots,B_{k}\) with integers entries such that \(A=B_{1}\cdot B_{2}\cdot \dots\cdot B_{k}\) and \(detB_{i}=b_{i}\) for all \(i=1,\dots,k\).
